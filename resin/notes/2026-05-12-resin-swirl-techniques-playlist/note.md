## Technique Reference

### 1. Pour-Down-the-Sides Technique

This method uses the inner wall of a vertical mold to guide resin gently downward, preserving color separation and preventing muddy blending.

**Purpose**
- Maintain clean, distinct color ribbons
- Reduce turbulence during pour
- Ideal for high-contrast blanks such as red, black, and white

**Reference Video**
- Title: Swirl Resin Without Blending Colors - Jake Thompson
- Platform: YouTube
- Technique Demonstrated: Controlled laminar flow, wall-guided pouring, viscosity timing

### 2. Center-Pour Ribbon Technique

A method where thickened resin is poured directly through the center of the mold to create bold, ribbon-like streaks.

**Purpose**
- Produce dramatic, high-contrast ribbons
- Use resin viscosity to control swirl integrity
- Works well with metallic or pearlescent pigments

**Reference Video**
- Title: Creating & Keeping Swirls in Resin - Nick Zammeti
- Platform: YouTube
- Technique Demonstrated: Center-stream pouring, timing for warm honey viscosity

### 3. Layer-Stack Technique

A sequential layering method used to create clean, distinct resin bands or controlled gradients.

**Purpose**
- Achieve crisp, non-blended layers
- Useful for segmented or striped blanks
- Allows precise color placement

**Reference Video**
- Title: How Do You Pour Multiple Resin Layers? - ArtResin
- Platform: YouTube
- Technique Demonstrated: Layer curing, sequential pours, color isolation

### 4. Vertical Casting & Swirl Behavior

A long-form demonstration covering vertical tube behavior, swirl timing, and color control.
