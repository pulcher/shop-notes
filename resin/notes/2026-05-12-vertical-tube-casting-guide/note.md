# Vertical Tube Casting Guide

## Overview

A reference for casting resin pen blanks in vertical PVC tubes.

---

## Reference

### Tube Prep
- Use **3/4" ID PVC**, cut to **6.25"**  
- Seal bottom with hot glue or HDPE caps  
- Spray interior with **Stoner Urethane Mold Release**  
- Pre‑warm tubes to **90–100°F**

### Resin Prep
- Use **Alumilite Clear Slow** for swirl control  
- Mix at **1:1 by weight**  
- Warm resin to **95–100°F** before mixing  

### Pour Techniques

#### Pour‑Down‑the‑Sides
- Run resin along the tube wall  
- Produces clean, separated ribbons  
- Best for high‑contrast colors

#### Center‑Pour Ribbon
- Pour thickened resin directly into center  
- Creates bold, dramatic streaks  
- Works well with metallic pigments

#### Layer‑Stack
- Pour sequential layers  
- Allow each to reach tacky stage  
- Produces crisp stripes or segments

### Timing
- Wait **8–12 minutes** after mixing  
- Resin should reach **warm honey** viscosity  
- Controls swirl definition

### Pressure Pot
- **50–60 PSI**  
- 2–3 hours minimum  
- Full cure: **12–24 hours**

---

## Tips & Warnings

### Muddy Colors
- Too much pigment  
- Poured too early  
- Colors collided in center

### Weak Swirls
- Resin too thin  
- Over‑mixed  
- Pigment too light

### Bubbles
- Resin or tubes too cold  
- Insufficient pressure  
- No mold release

---

## Tools / Materials

- 3/4" ID PVC tubes (6.25" length)  
- Hot glue or HDPE caps  
- Stoner Urethane Mold Release  
- Alumilite Clear Slow  
- Precision scale  
- Pressure pot  

---

## Notes

- Vertical tubes create long ribboning naturally  
- Pour order dramatically affects final pattern  
- Pre‑warming tubes reduces wall‑chill bubbles
