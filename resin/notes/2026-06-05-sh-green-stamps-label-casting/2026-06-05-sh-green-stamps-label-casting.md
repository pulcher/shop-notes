# S&H Green Stamps Label Casting

*Vintage stamps · Glue stick adhesion · Mod Podge edge seal · Alumilite Clear*

## 1 · Flatten & Prepare Stamps

> Vintage S&H stamps must be stabilized before wrapping.

- Press stamps between printer paper under a heavy book for **1–2 hours**
- Lightly warm with a hair dryer **5–10 sec** if curled
- Trim excess paper but keep perforations intact

## 2 · Paint & Prep the Brass Tube

> A smooth, resin-safe base coat prevents bubbles and lift.

- Scuff tube with **600–800 grit**
- Wipe with **91% isopropyl alcohol**
- Brush on FolkArt Matte or Golden High Flow (thin coats)
- Heat-set paint **1–2 min** and cool fully

## 3 · Apply Glue Stick Adhesive

> Elmer's Purple Glue Stick gives smooth adhesion without wrinkles.

- Apply one thin, even coat to the tube (light lavender haze)
- Avoid clumps, ridges, or shiny wet spots
- Tube should feel **tacky, not wet**

## 4 · Wrap & Burnish the Stamp Strip

> Correct alignment and pressure prevent air pockets and resin creep.

- Align first stamp (diagonal or straight) and anchor it
- Wrap with gentle, even tension
- Burnish firmly, especially perforations
- Wait **2–3 minutes**, then burnish again

## 5 · Seal Perforated Edges with Mod Podge

> ⚠️ **Critical Step** — A micro-thin edge seal prevents resin from wicking under the perforations.

- Use **Mod Podge Matte** with a fine liner brush
- Wipe almost all off → apply a hairline bead along perforations
- Do **not** fill holes or coat the stamp face
- Heat-set **20–30 sec**, then cool **1–2 min**

## 6 · Cast with Alumilite Clear

> Proper temperature and pressure ensure bubble-free results.

- Warm resin to **85–90°F**
- Pour slowly to avoid turbulence
- Pressure pot at **50–60 PSI** until fully cured
- Allow full cure before turning
