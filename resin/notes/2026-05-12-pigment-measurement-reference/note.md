## Overview

A practical guide for measuring mica pigments by weight for resin casting.

---

## Reference

### Micro‑Scoop Equivalents
A typical mica micro‑scoop holds:
- **0.02–0.05 g**  
- Average: **0.03 g**

Kitchen measurement equivalents:
- 1/16 tsp ≈ 0.30 g  
- 1/8 tsp ≈ 0.60 g  
- A "pinch" ≈ 0.10 g  

**Conclusion:**  
A micro‑scoop is **1/10–1/20 of a 1/16 teaspoon**.

---

### Recommended Pigment Weights (per 60–70 g resin cup)
- **Low opacity:** 0.05–0.08 g  
- **Standard opacity:** 0.10–0.15 g  
- **High shimmer:** 0.15–0.25 g  
- **Interference powders:** 0.01–0.03 g  

---

### Why Weight Beats Scoops
- Repeatable  
- Scales with batch size  
- Prevents over‑loading (which kills swirl motion)  
- Works across brands (Eye Candy, Black Diamond, etc.)

---

## Tips & Warnings

- Too much mica thickens resin prematurely  
- Black pigments overpower easily  
- White pigments require more for opacity  

---

## Tools / Materials

- 0.01 g precision scale  
- Anti‑static brush  
- Small pigment cups  
- Toothpick for micro‑adjustments
