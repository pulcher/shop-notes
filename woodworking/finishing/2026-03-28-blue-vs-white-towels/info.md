# Blue vs. White Paper Towels in Wood, Resin & Hybrid Turning
### Finish‑by‑Finish Reference Guide

## Blue Shop Towels — When They’re Best
- Stronger when wet
- Low lint
- Heat‑resistant
- Ideal for resin, CA glue, wet sanding, and friction polishing

## White Paper Towels — When They’re Best
- Softer on wood
- More absorbent
- Better tactile feedback
- Ideal for oils, waxes, sanding dust, and final buffing

---

# Finish‑by‑Finish Breakdown

## CA Glue
Use: Blue towels only  
White towels fuse into CA instantly.

## Oil Finishes
Use: White towels for application  
Use: Blue towels for friction polishing

## Wax Finishes
Use: White towels  
Blue towels may leave swirl marks.

## Friction Polishes
Use: White towels for application  
Use: Blue towels for buffing

## Resin Polishing Compounds
Use: Blue towels only  
White towels leave lint that shows in gloss.

## Yorkshire Grit
Use: Blue towels for application  
Use: White towels for final buff

## Sanding Sealer
Use: White towels  
Blue towels only for resin-heavy hybrids.

## Polishing Compounds
Use: Blue towels  
White towels for final wipe-down.

# Quick Reference Table

| Finish Type         | Blue Towels | White Towels |
|---------------------|-------------|--------------|
| CA Glue             | Required    | Never        |
| Oil Finishes        | Optional    | Best         |
| Wax Finishes        | Sometimes   | Best         |
| Friction Polishes   | Buffing     | Application  |
| Resin Polishes      | Required    | No           |
| Yorkshire Grit      | Application | Final buff   |
| Sanding Sealer      | Hybrids     | Wood         |
| Polishing Compounds | Best        | Final wipe   |

---

# Material‑Specific Notes

### Wood
White towels for most tasks; blue for CA or wet sanding.

### Resin
Blue towels only.

### Hybrid Blanks
Blue for most work; white only for final buff on wood sections.
